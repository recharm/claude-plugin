# Hook Templates Reference

A comprehensive bank of hook archetypes for ad creative. Organized into categories — when building 10 hooks for a brand, select across at least 7 different categories for variety.

All templates use `[X]` or `___` as fill-in placeholders. Always replace every placeholder with specific, brand-relevant language before using.

---

## Fill-In Guidance

When adapting any template for a brand, replace placeholders concretely:

- `[pain point]` → the specific frustration your audience feels before finding the brand (e.g. "energy crashes by 2pm", "cereal that tastes like cardboard")
- `[outcome]` → the desirable state the product delivers (e.g. "feel full for hours", "hit your protein without sacrificing flavor")
- `[product]` → the product name or the category descriptor (e.g. "Magic Spoon", "high-protein cereal")
- `[niche]` → the audience identity or lifestyle (e.g. "fitness", "keto", "busy parents")
- `[timeframe]` → a believable but impressive duration (e.g. "2 weeks", "30 days", "one bowl")
- `[#]` → a specific number (e.g. "3", "5", "7")
- `[audience]` → who you're speaking to (e.g. "women 25–45", "keto dieters", "gym-goers")

**Diversity rule:** Pick at most 1–2 hooks per category. A good 10-hook brief spans at least 7 different categories.

**Visual pairing:** Each hook type naturally suggests a visual opening:

- Question hooks → a relatable "before" scene or someone mid-struggle
- Social proof hooks → a before/after, product in use, or results visual
- POV/lifestyle hooks → first-person product interaction
- Belief breaker hooks → something visually unexpected or dramatic
- How-to hooks → hands demonstrating a step, product being prepared
- FOMO hooks → product reveal, unboxing, or crowd/social proof visual
- Announce hooks → product hero shot, packaging, or new arrival reveal
